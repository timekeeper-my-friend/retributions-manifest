**Description:**
_____________
Circle 1 models a 21st century American metro system in both layout, and architectural appearance. It is made up of two floors consisting of materials visually similar to heavy-duty porcelain tiles and industry grade resin. Despite an estimated hundreds of years of habitation, and 198 years of observation, no physical damage has been observed on any structural material of the building, and under testing, it seems to take tools from Circle |REDACTED| to scratch, showing a hardness much above diamond on the Mohs Hardness Scale. Any small particulate collected bears no molecular difference to earth-grade material. It is estimated that any heavy grade construction material produced locally in the circle is fortified, however, imported material is not affected by this phenomenon. 

The first, 'ground' floor is a platform with an infinite number of rail lines running parallel to each other, with the platform also running infinitely beside them. The second floor is made up of shops and food courts running perpendicular to the rail lines. On one side, there is a fence overlooking the first floor, with staircases roughly every mile, and on the other side, food courts and shops lined up in a similar fashion to a modern American strip mall. 

Contents of novelty shops consist of generic, and unbranded merchandise. Examples such as plastic sun-glasses in various colors, pins stating phrases such as "Welcome to the Metro", "First-time traveler", and "I Heart Metro". Other merchandise observed included backpacks, unbranded water and snacks, as well as journals and writing utensils. There even seems to be a location of a "Waffle House", A well known South-East American brand. This building, despite being a wanderer outpost, is not advisable to visit, as the wanderers within are quite dangerous and aggressive compared to other wanderers in Manifest.

Despite the infinite nature of the station, there are traditional rail signs and floor markings on the second floor. The signs show integers, counting upwards every sign moved right, and counting downwards for every sign moved left, interspersed of a space of about 100 feet, allowing for finding location within the station. There are also moving walkways, allowing for faster travel through the circle. For wanderers within the station, some safe outposts include:

**Infrastructure and Survival:**
____________________________
Within the circle, Wi-Fi systems have been set up, allowing for intra-circle communication using imported routers and computers. This allows for online viewing of documents such as this one,  communication, and possibly leisure. Sadly, any Wi-Fi set up within the circle will not transfer to the outside world, thus making communication only possible through physical via Pockets.

Food and water should be obtainable via the shops and food courts, although while on a train, there is a train car dedicated to food. According to the currently explored regions of the circle, there are two major distribution centers that get regular supply shipments. It is unknown where these shipments come from, but they come every Sunday. These centers act as regional suppliers, giving supplies such as perishable goods, and restocking material for store-fronts between them and the other suppliers. Foods given have varied in origin, coming from different circles, but they have always been safe consumables. The economy and law of the circle is governed primarily by the Wanderer's Coalition, working in tandem with G.R.A.C.E. for certain infrastructure.

**Circle Specific Information**
_____________
The circle's trains are perhaps its most identifiable feature. These trains can be seemingly any model, from a steam locomotive, to a monorail, to a maglev. Due to this, as a train is passing through, the tracks will change to suit the type of the train. Every train will stay at their platform for 2 minutes, although they enter and leave at different times. The most famous capability of these trains is to move between circles. These circles are chosen seemingly randomly, and use the name of the circle that the passenger knows, displayed on a screen saying "Next Stop: -----". If multiple passengers are on the same train, they will each see the name of the circle that they know best. If they do not know the circle, the screen will simply show question marks, although if multiple people are on the train, it will show the majority knowledge. The trains will take 30-60 seconds to move from a platform on Circle 1 to a platform on a different circle. Many people see these trains as beacons of salvation, as stations for these trains can appear at random in a random place, meaning that if one sees a train station appear while in a dangerous circle, they can get on the train and get back to Circle 1.
